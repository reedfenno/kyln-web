01. THE PROBLEM

The Actinide Gap

Relativistic f-electron chemistry is notoriously difficult. Data is scarce, experiments are hazardous, and computational costs for high-level DFT (ZORA/CASSCF) are prohibitive.

  • Strong correlation
  • Relativistic effects
  • Sparse data

02. THE SOLUTION

Transfer Learning

We leverage abundant organic data (ANI-2x) to learn physics, transfer to transition metals (tmQM), and fine-tune on scarce actinides using E(3)-equivariant networks.

E = Eorganic + ΔEmetal + ΔErel

03. THE LOOP

Active Discovery

A closed-loop system: Forge generates ligands, Kilonova predicts stability with uncertainty, and Reactor orchestrates DFT verification for high-uncertainty candidates.

Generate → Predict → Verify

Development Roadmap

Phase 1

Foundation Pre-training

Training KilonovaGNN on 9M organic conformations (ANI-2x). Learning general molecular physics and atomic interactions.

Status: Complete
Phase 2

Transfer Fine-tuning

Adapting the foundation model to transition metals (tmQM). Solving the "Z-mismatch" via embedding surgery.

Status: In Progress
Phase 3

Autonomous Actinide Discovery

The Reactor active learning loop. Generation, verification (ORCA/CASSCF), and retraining on relativistic systems.

Status: Planned
