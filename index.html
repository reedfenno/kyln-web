The Chelation Bottleneck

Research Thesis

01. The Clinical Problem

Legacy Chemistry

Targeted Alpha Therapy (TAT) delivers unmatched clinical potency by utilizing heavy alpha particles to induce irreparable double-strand DNA breaks over a highly localized cellular radius (high LET). Despite this massive therapeutic window over traditional beta-emitters, the field relies on legacy chelators that were never computationally optimized for massive, radioactive ions. This reliance on non-specific geometry often leads to in vivo instability, transchelation, and off-target toxicity.

02. The Physical Challenge

Relativistic Complexity

Designing ligands for heavy elements requires modeling complex f-block coordination challenges and profound scalar relativistic effects (e.g., core orbital contraction). Standard computational tools fail, and high-fidelity multi-reference quantum mechanics is too computationally expensive for high-throughput screening.

03. The Kyln Solution

Closed-Loop Discovery

Project Kyln maps proprietary geometric deep learning to conservative force fields and rigorous quantum mechanics. The system autonomously generates and evaluates massive, pre-organized macrocycles that encapsulate alpha-emitters with high kinetic inertness.

The Scale of Chemical Space

Discovering optimal chelators is a profound search problem. For a 20-membered macrocycle with mixed N/O/S donors, the combinatorial space of possible highly-strained scaffolds exceeds 1015. Traditional screening utilizing functional-grade Density Functional Theory (DFT) to map the potential energy surface of these massive complexes would require centuries of compute. Project Kyln evaluates physically constrained candidates at 100,000× the throughput of quantum mechanics while maintaining sub-kcal/mol fidelity.

Model Validation & Precision

To reliably design novel chelators, a computational engine must predict binding energetics with extreme precision. The universally accepted threshold for "chemical accuracy" in computational chemistry is 1 kcal/mol, which equates to roughly 43.4 meV.

Evaluated against a strict hold-out validation set of unseen metal-ligand graphs, the Kilonova AI engine achieves a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 6.64 meV/atom, securing true first-principles molecular design capability without the computational tax of raw DFT.

Known Limitations & Domain Extrapolation

Because high-fidelity, multi-reference quantum data for heavy actinides is virtually nonexistent, computational engines face a profound domain gap. Extrapolating predictions to f-block targets relies entirely on a network's mathematical capacity to generalize complex local chemical environments and profound scalar relativistic effects from lighter reference data. To systematically overcome this limitation, Project Kyln deploys a proprietary closed-loop verification engine. The system autonomously directs high-fidelity computational oracles to sample the most informative regions of the actinide potential energy surface, enabling rapid, targeted domain adaptation.
